CCDesigner for We Teach Drama – Video Walkthrough Script

Purpose: Recording script for a product demo video aimed at We Teach Drama. Use this while screen-recording the platform so you cover every feature and connect each one to how it helps We Teach Drama deliver teaching resources, lesson plans, webinars, and courses.

We Teach Drama context (from their site):

  • Award-winning drama teaching resources; 5-star on-demand digital resources and CPD videos.
  • Courses: Teach Stanislavski, Teach Artaud, Teach Theatre Design, Key Stage 3 Masterplan.
  • Live webinars: Curriculum Clinic, Drama Starters to Ignite Learning (Zoom, recorded, replays 30 days).
  • Free resources and blog; classroom-ready content for drama teachers (UK and international).
  • Founded by Lucy Bellingham; drama consultants and trainers.

Suggested video length: 20–30 minutes (or split into 2–3 shorter clips: Activity bank & lessons; Units, stacks & calendar; Export, share & settings).

OPENING (30–60 seconds)

[Screen: Login or home dashboard with We Teach Drama branding if already set]

“Hi, I’m [your name]. This is a walkthrough of CCDesigner – the lesson-planning platform we’re piloting with We Teach Drama.

We Teach Drama already delivers high-impact CPD, classroom-ready resources, on-demand courses like Teach Stanislavski and Teach Theatre Design, and live webinars such as Curriculum Clinic and Drama Starters. CCDesigner is designed to help you *build, organise, and reuse* your drama activities and lesson plans in one place – so your team can create consistent resources for teachers, webinars, and courses without starting from scratch every time.

I’ll show you every part of the platform and how it fits how you work.”

1. MAIN TABS AND NAVIGATION (1 min)

[Screen: Dashboard with all five tabs visible]

“After you log in, you see five main areas: Unit Viewer, Lesson Library, Lesson Builder, Activity Library, and Calendar.

  • Unit Viewer – how your teaching is organised by half-term; you can see which lessons sit in Autumn 1, Spring 2, and so on. Great for mapping a course or a term’s scheme of work.
  • Lesson Library – all your lesson plans in one place; create new ones, duplicate, or copy to another class or year group.
  • Lesson Builder – where you build or edit a lesson: add activities, set learning goals, add notes and structure.
  • Activity Library – your bank of reusable drama activities, exercises, and teaching strategies. This is the foundation everything else is built from.
  • Calendar – week and month view; assign lesson plans to specific dates. Useful for planning webinar content or course schedules.”

[Click through each tab briefly so the viewer sees the layout.]

2. ACTIVITY LIBRARY – YOUR REUSABLE DRAMA BANK (4–5 min)

[Screen: Activity Library]

“The Activity Library is where you store every drama activity, warm-up, exercise, or teaching strategy you use. Instead of digging through old plans or notes, you keep them here once and reuse them in any lesson, course, or webinar.

Adding an activity

Click Add Activity. You get: a title, a rich-text description so you can add instructions and formatting, duration, and a category – you can create categories that match how you work, for example ‘Warm-ups’, ‘Stanislavski techniques’, ‘Theatre design tasks’. You can also assign activities to year groups or key stages so the right content shows up for the right course.

Resources and links

Each activity can have links to video, music, backing tracks, resources, vocals, images, or Canva designs. So if an activity uses a specific clip from your CPD videos or a worksheet from Teach Theatre Design, you attach it here. Those links will stay clickable when you export a lesson plan as a PDF – so teachers or trainees can open the resource with one click during a webinar or in the classroom.

Search and filter

Use search and filters to find activities by name, category, or year group. When you’re building a lesson for a webinar or a course module, you can quickly pull in the right activities without re-typing anything.

Import

If you already have activities in a spreadsheet or list, you can import them via the Data and backup settings, so you don’t have to enter everything by hand.”

[Optional: show one activity being created and one being found via search.]

3. LESSON LIBRARY AND BUILDING LESSONS (4–5 min)

[Screen: Lesson Library]

“The Lesson Library holds all your lesson plans. Each plan is built from activities you’ve already saved in the Activity Library.

Creating a lesson

Click Create Lesson. You get a lesson builder where you add activities in the order you want – drag and drop or pick from your library. You can add learning goals – and here’s where your custom curriculum comes in: you can set up drama-focused objectives or key stages in Settings, then attach them to lessons so each plan is clearly aligned to what you teach, whether that’s KS3, exam specs, or your own framework.

You can add lesson notes – perfect for presenter notes for a webinar or reminders for a course session. You can also set a custom header and footer for that lesson, which will appear when you export to PDF, so you can brand it for We Teach Drama or for a specific course.

Duplicate and copy

If you run similar sessions across different courses or year groups, you can duplicate a lesson and tweak it, or copy lessons to another class so you don’t rebuild the same plan again.

Trash and restore

If you delete a lesson by mistake, you can open the Trash view and restore it.”

[Screen: Lesson Builder or Standalone Lesson Creator]

“So when you’re preparing a webinar like Drama Starters or a module for Teach Theatre Design, you’re not writing from scratch – you’re pulling from your activity bank, ordering the flow, adding learning goals and notes, and saving it as a reusable lesson plan.”

4. UNITS, HALF-TERMS AND LESSON STACKS (3–4 min)

[Screen: Unit Viewer]

“The Unit Viewer shows your teaching organised by half-terms – Autumn 1 and 2, Spring 1 and 2, Summer 1 and 2. You can assign lessons to each half-term so you see exactly what you’re covering in each block. That’s useful for structuring a term’s scheme of work or a course like the Key Stage 3 Masterplan.

Lesson stacks

You can also group lessons into stacks – for example ‘Stanislavski introduction’ or ‘Theatre design – set and lighting’. A stack is a named bundle of lessons you can assign to a half-term or reuse across courses. So you might have a stack for each module of Teach Theatre Design, and assign those stacks to the right weeks or terms.

Copy term

If you teach the same structure in different year groups or cohorts, you can copy a term to another class so the assignments are duplicated without rebuilding.”

[Show assigning a lesson to a half-term and, if possible, a stack.]

5. CURRICULUM AND CUSTOM OBJECTIVES (2–3 min)

[Screen: Settings → Custom Objectives]

“Under Settings, Custom Objectives lets you define your own curriculum framework. You can create areas – for example ‘Devising’, ‘Performance’, ‘Theatre design’ – and under each area add objectives or statements that match your courses and exam boards.

Once they’re set up, you can attach these objectives to lessons and to activities. So when you export a lesson plan as a PDF or share it with teachers, it’s clear which objectives that lesson or activity supports. That helps with course documentation, CPD evidence, and making sure webinars and resources align with what schools and trainees need.”

6. EXPORT PDF AND SHAREABLE LINKS (3–4 min)

[Screen: Lesson Library – open a lesson, show Export PDF and Copy Link]

“Every lesson plan can be exported as a PDF. The PDF includes the lesson header, learning goals, all activities in order with their descriptions, and any links you’ve added – and those links stay clickable in the PDF. So when you send the plan to teachers or use it in a webinar, they can click through to videos, worksheets, or resources without retyping URLs.

Copy Link creates a shareable link to a read-only version of that lesson plan. You can paste it in an email, in your learning platform, or in webinar follow-up materials. Recipients open the link and see the full plan in their browser – no login required for them. That’s ideal for sharing resources after a webinar or giving course participants access to session plans.

You can also export a whole half-term or unit as one PDF, and there’s a calendar export so you can share a week or month view if that’s useful for planning.”

[Demonstrate: export one lesson as PDF, show clickable link; optionally generate a Copy Link and open it in a new tab.]

7. CALENDAR (2 min)

[Screen: Calendar]

“The Calendar gives you a week or month view. You can assign lesson plans to specific dates – so you can plan which webinar or course session uses which lesson on which day. You can create a new plan from a date or attach an existing lesson to a date. And you can export the calendar view as PDF if you want to share a schedule with your team or with course participants.”

8. TRADITIONAL LESSON PLAN FORMAT (1–2 min)

[Screen: Lesson plan details in a lesson – learning outcome, main activity, plenary, etc.]

“As well as the activity-based layout, CCDesigner supports a traditional lesson plan format with sections like learning outcome, success criteria, introduction, main activity, plenary, vocabulary, key questions, resources, differentiation, and assessment. So when you need a more formal plan – for a school, a course submission, or a CPD record – you can fill those in and they’ll appear in the export. You get both: quick building from activities and detailed sections when you need them.”

9. SETTINGS, BRANDING AND ADMIN (2–3 min)

[Screen: Settings – Year groups, Categories, Admin → Branding]

“Under Settings you can configure year groups or classes – so if your courses use key stages or year groups, you set them here and they appear across the app. Categories for activities are customisable too – add the ones that match your content, like ‘Warm-ups’, ‘Practitioner techniques’, or ‘Design tasks’.

Branding – in the Admin area – is where you set the login title (e.g. ‘We Teach Drama – Lesson Planner’), your logo, footer text, and company name that appear on PDFs and shared links. So everything teachers and trainees see can carry the We Teach Drama branding.

User management – if you have the right role – lets you invite team members and set who can edit and who can only view, so you can control who builds content for courses and webinars.”

10. HOW THIS HELPS WE TEACH DRAMA (1–2 min)

[Screen: Dashboard or a summary view]

“So in practice:

  • Resources and shop: The activities you sell or give away can be built and stored in the Activity Library, then turned into lesson plans that you export as PDFs or share via link – so teachers get consistent, professional plans with clickable resources.
  • On-demand courses: For Teach Stanislavski, Teach Artaud, Teach Theatre Design, or the KS3 Masterplan, you can build a bank of activities and lessons per module, organise them in half-terms and stacks, and export or share them as part of the course materials.
  • Webinars: For each webinar – Curriculum Clinic, Drama Starters, or others – you can create a lesson plan that matches the session, attach links to any videos or resources you use, and after the webinar send participants a shareable link or PDF so they have the plan and links in one place.
  • CPD and consistency: Your team can reuse the same activities and plans across different courses and cohorts, so your CPD and resources stay consistent and nothing gets lost in old documents or emails.”

CLOSING (30–60 seconds)

[Screen: Back to dashboard or login]

“That’s CCDesigner – a single place to build, organise, and share your drama teaching resources and lesson plans for teachers, webinars, and courses. If you’d like to try it with your team or talk about how it could be set up for We Teach Drama, get in touch. Thanks for watching.”
