
**Your journey & philosophy**
- You've been teaching for over 20 years and you're also a founder and builder — how do those two identities feed each other? Does being in the classroom make you a better product designer?
- You're passionate about arts lessons never becoming passive experiences. What does that look like in practice — what's the worst version of a music or drama lesson, and what's the best?

**The EdTech tools**
- What problem frustrated you enough that you decided to build Assessify yourself rather than just live with what existed?
- Assessment in the arts feels inherently tricky — how do you quantify creativity without crushing it? How does Assessify handle that tension?
- PeriFeedback addresses peripatetic teaching, which is quite niche — tell us about that world and why it needed its own tool.

**CCDesigner & the bigger vision**
- You've described a gap between organisations like the LSO and Royal Opera House producing brilliant resources, and teachers actually using them. Why does that gap exist, and why hasn't it been solved before?
- I've been thinking about Stacks almost like wiki pages — living documents that evolve as more teachers use and contribute to them. Is that a fair mental model? And if so, how do you stop them becoming messy or diluted over time, the way wikis sometimes do?
- Who has editorial control over a Stack — the person who created it, the school, or the community?
- Do you see teachers actually wanting to share and contribute, or is there a risk of a "free rider" problem where everyone consumes but few build?
- Is there a Wikipedia moment you're hoping for — where a critical mass of contributions suddenly makes the whole thing dramatically more valuable?
- You're applying to the Arts Council for support. What does success look like for CCDesigner in five years — is it a free public good, a sustainable business, or something else?

**AI in arts education**
- There's a lot of anxiety about AI in schools right now. You seem to sit in a specific position — using AI to support teachers without automating the artistry itself. Where exactly is that line for you?
- What's a use of AI in education that genuinely excites you, and one that genuinely worries you?

**The state of arts education**
- Drama and music are often the first casualties when schools face budget pressure. Are you optimistic or pessimistic about the next ten years for arts education in the UK?
- What do you wish school leaders understood about what happens in your department that they currently don't?
