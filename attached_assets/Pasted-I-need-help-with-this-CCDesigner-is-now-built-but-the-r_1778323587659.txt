CCDesigner is now built, but the real vision behind it has always depended on collaboration rather than profit.

CCDesigner is a creative curriculum and lesson design platform built specifically for drama, dance, music and arts-rich learning. It allows teachers to create, save and build upon their own ideas over time rather than constantly starting from scratch. The aim is to give educators a living creative workspace where rehearsal techniques, warm-ups, activities, adaptations, reflections and curriculum ideas can continue evolving instead of disappearing into forgotten folders and isolated planning documents.

I’m currently applying for Arts Council support because I genuinely believe we need a much bigger conversation about the future of arts education and how we support teachers creatively over the long term.

Too many brilliant ideas disappear in education while teachers are left exhausted trying to reinvent creativity alone. At the same time, there are extraordinary organisations such as LSO, ROH and many others doing powerful work to keep the arts alive for children despite increasing pressure on schools and shrinking creative opportunities.

What worries me is that so much of this work still exists separately.

CCDesigner was created to become a shared creative ecosystem where arts organisations, practitioners, outreach teams and educators can contribute ideas, approaches and inspiration openly so they continue evolving rather than disappearing once projects end.

The aim is not to sell resources back to teachers.

The aim is to create a genuine melting pot of creative practice that supports:

- arts inclusion for all children
- non-specialist teachers who need inspiration and confidence
- stronger links between schools and arts organisations
- long-term sharing of creative approaches and planning
- meaningful drama, dance and music experiences across KS2 and KS3
- collaboration instead of isolated projects

I don’t want children growing up believing the arts are less valuable because access depends on postcode, funding or specialist staffing.

I want creativity to feel connected, ambitious and accessible again.

If you work in arts education, outreach, theatre, dance, music, curriculum or creative learning, I would genuinely love to connect and discuss how organisations can help shape and support this vision moving forward.
