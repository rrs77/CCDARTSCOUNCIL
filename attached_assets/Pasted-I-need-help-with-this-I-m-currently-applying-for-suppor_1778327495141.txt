I need help with this.

I’m currently applying for support from the Arts Council because I genuinely believe we need a much bigger conversation about the future of arts education and how we support both teachers and children creatively over the long term.

Over the past few years I’ve watched passionate teachers work themselves into exhaustion trying to keep creativity alive in schools while curriculum time narrows, funding becomes harder to access and increasing pressure pushes creative subjects further towards the edges of education. Too often the arts survive because of individual determination rather than because the system genuinely protects them.

At the same time, there are extraordinary organisations across the arts sector doing inspiring work with schools and young people. Organisations like LSO, ROH and many others continue to create ambitious outreach projects, workshops and educational opportunities despite all the challenges the sector faces. The problem is not a lack of passion or expertise. The problem is that so much of this work still exists in separate spaces while teachers themselves are left trying to navigate creativity alone.

CCDesigner grew from this frustration.

It was never intended to become a profit-making app or another commercial resource platform. The idea behind it is much more human than that. I wanted to create a shared creative space where ideas do not disappear once projects end, funding runs out or planning gets buried inside forgotten folders. Teachers create brilliant things every single day — rehearsal techniques, drama activities, creative adaptations, musical ideas, warm-ups, reflective notes and moments of genuine inspiration — yet most of it is lost because there is no long-term space for those ideas to evolve and connect.

The vision for CCDesigner is to create a living creative ecosystem where teachers, arts organisations, outreach teams, practitioners and training providers can contribute ideas openly, revisit them over time and build on one another’s creative practice rather than constantly starting from scratch.

What matters most to me is ensuring that creativity becomes more accessible rather than more fragmented. I don’t want schools without specialist provision to feel excluded from meaningful arts experiences. I don’t want non-specialist teachers to feel intimidated or unsupported when trying to deliver drama, dance or music. I don’t want children reaching KS3 believing the arts are somehow less valuable because they are no longer treated as serious pathways within education.

The arts matter deeply because they shape confidence, empathy, communication, imagination and identity in ways that cannot always be measured easily.

The long-term ambition for CCDesigner is centred around:

* bringing arts organisations and educators together into one collaborative creative space
* helping teachers build long-term archives of evolving creative practice
* supporting non-specialist teachers with accessible inspiration and adaptable ideas
* ensuring children from all backgrounds can access ambitious creative learning
* connecting outreach work and classroom practice more meaningfully
* creating shared spaces where creative approaches continue growing instead of disappearing after short-term projects end
* encouraging collaboration across drama, dance, music and cross-curricular arts learning
* protecting creativity as a serious and essential part of education rather than an optional extra

I genuinely believe we need to stop working in isolated pockets and start building something collectively.

If you work in arts education, theatre outreach, music, dance, curriculum development, teacher training or creative learning, I would genuinely love to connect and continue these conversations because I think there is a real opportunity to rethink how creative ideas are shared, supported and sustained in schools.
