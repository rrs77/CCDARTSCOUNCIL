A few things are working:

the colour palette
the clean UI
the sense of polish
the “presentation-ready” concept

But the messaging, pacing, visuals, and structure are currently sending the wrong signal.

Here’s the core issue:

It looks like software for administrators.
It should feel like a living creative curriculum environment.

Right now the slides are saying:

“professional”
“planning”
“systems”
“documents”

But your real value is:

shared creative practice
arts education support
resource ecosystems
adaptable lesson design
collaboration between educators and organisations
high-quality practical teaching content
visual/music/drama-rich curriculum creation

And most importantly:

You need evidence, not claims.

The promo currently states things.
It needs to show things.

Main Problems
1. Text Scale Is Too Aggressive

The oversized typography works for startup landing pages, but not for this audience.

“Beautiful, professional plans.”

feels:

corporate
vague
sales-y

Instead:

smaller
more editorial
more cinematic
more purposeful

The audience is educators + arts organisations.
They respond better to:

authenticity
practical usefulness
visible pedagogy
creative energy

Not “enterprise software” language.

2. Repeated Screenshots Reduce Credibility

Repeated screens instantly make the product feel unfinished.

Especially in a promo trying to build trust.

You need:

lots of unique app moments
real workflows
real lesson content
actual planning examples
real curriculum materials

Even partially blurred/fake sample data is better than repetition.

3. Missing the “Creative Education” Identity

At the moment:

there’s no music
no drama
no visual arts
no classroom energy
no student outcomes
no creative process

It could be:

payroll software
HR software
CRM software

You need:

lesson excerpts
notation
activity cards
rehearsal plans
performance prep
teacher annotations
visual curriculum maps
embedded media
drag/drop planning
resource sharing
arts vocabulary
progression examples
4. “AI” Should Be Removed Completely

If you are not using AI yet:

don’t imply it
don’t visually hint at it
don’t use trendy AI motion graphics

Arts organisations especially are increasingly sceptical of fake “AI-enhanced” education claims.

You’ll gain more trust by positioning it as:

educator-designed
curriculum-informed
practical
collaborative
resource-rich

That’s actually stronger.

5. Too Many Generic Business Graphics

These weaken the product.

Things to remove:

floating documents
generic glowing UI cards
abstract productivity visuals
fake dashboards
meaningless icon animations

Instead show:

real app interaction
actual lesson editing
curriculum timelines
media embedding
resources opening
teachers dragging activities
music/drama/arts examples
printable outputs
classroom-ready materials
6. Need More Motion + Layering

Since this is primarily desktop viewed:
you can lean much harder into:

cinematic motion
depth
layered UI
parallax
stacked interfaces
animated workflows

Especially:

stacked lesson plans
cascading resources
expanding curriculum maps
draggable activity cards
animated progression pathways
linked media opening dynamically

Right now the motion feels static.

You want:

creative software energy
not presentation slides
7. “Proof” Is Missing

This is the biggest issue.

The promo says:

“professional”
“beautiful”
“share confidently”

But doesn’t prove it.

You need sequences like:

BEFORE → AFTER

Messy planning docs
↓
RhythmStix organised curriculum map
↓
Lesson resources linked
↓
Printable polished outcome

OR:

Music activity card
↓
Embedded song
↓
Rhythm game
↓
Printable notation
↓
Lesson export

OR:

Arts organisation uploads workshop resources
↓
Teachers adapt them
↓
Curriculum-linked lessons generated
↓
Shared resource ecosystem grows

That tells the real story.
